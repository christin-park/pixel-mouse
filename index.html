Project Goal

TAMU IEEE Game Controllers is a team-based project that allows members to learn new electrical engineering concepts based on their current skill level. There are 2 categories of what you can create as a member-- a breadboard or a PCB controller. The lectures cover information on introductory electrical engineering concepts, Arduino, button/joystick implementation, soldering, and PCB design. Participating members will form groups with one team leader to complete the design.

Why are we making game controllers? Pixel Mouse (Learn about Pixel Mouse) is a custom-made timed maze game that teams can compete in for a prize. A comprehensive list of the hotkeys coded into the game can guide you in how you implement the game controller, and a variety of hardware is introduced into the lectures to help you make a unique design. After teams finish building their game controllers, we will host an end-of-semester competition to play Pixel Mouse! Gift cards will be given to the winners of the game, and the best technical design will give the team lead/representative a scholarship to the 2024 IEEE Rising Stars Conference!

This semester project is also an entry for the 2024 IEEE Rising Stars Conference's project showcase, an opportunity for student branches of IEEE to present an original technical project. Among the teams that opt into allowing their design to be presented, the best technical design will be showcased in the conference!
