Getting Started

Confused on whether to create a breadboard or PCB controller?

Comparisons

A comparisons on recommendations and content covered. (Note: These are just general recommendations; feel free to choose the project that you think best fits you). Your choice is not set in stone--if the majority of your team agrees on changing from one to the other throughout the project, you are free to do so!

  • No prerequisites!! Beginner-friendly introduction to electrical engineering for those in ETAM, deciding on a major, or wanting to learn a bit about electronics.
  • Teaches basic topics covered in ECEN 214.
  • Covers content over Arduinos, circuit theory, and prototyping/design.
  • Suited for members with some experience with breadboarding. (However, if you are up for a challenge as a beginner, feel free to create a PCB controller!)
  • All content is the same as the breadboard lectures with the addition of lectures over soldering and PCB design.
  • Access to Altium Designer, an industry standard PCB design software. (The PCB design lecture is planned to be taught by Prof. Lushner!)

Want to be a team leader?

As a team leader, your role will be to lead a team of 4-5 members to collaborately create either a breadboard or a PCB controller. Lectures are taught by one main member who will talk through PowerPoint slides. However, it is challenging to follow along with a lecture without hands-on experience. Team leaders will also demonstrate the topics taught in lecture by implementing the hardware/software shown in lectures. If you do not want to participate in the Pixel Mouse competition.
